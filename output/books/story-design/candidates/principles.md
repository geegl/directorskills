# principles.md — 原则/规则提取候选

---

id: p01
title: Save the Cat — 英雄认同建立术
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "The 'Save the Cat!' beat in any movie, novel, or story is that moment when we meet its hero and he does something 'nice' — like save a cat — that makes us like him and want to root for him."
summary: |
  英雄出场时做的一件让观众喜欢/认同的事。变体包括:
  "Kill the Cat"（英雄被同情而非被喜欢）、"Save the Cat by proxy"（通过他人推荐英雄）、
  "Kill the Cat by proxy"（有比英雄更坏的人衬托）。
  这是故事与观众建立连接的第一步——没有它,观众不会上车。
tags: [hero, identification, audience-connection]

id: p02
title: Theme Stated/B Story 轴心
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch2 Themes
source_quote: "Theme Stated/B Story must be addressed in your story. If your movie isn't 'about' something, why write it? And when you find out what it's about, say it, right up front."
summary: |
  主题必须在片头5页内通过一个看似不起眼的角色说出来(Theme Stated),然后通过 B Story(通常是爱情线)
  讨论和深化。B Story 是"Helper Story",帮助英雄理解他需要的改变。
  三步法:①State it up front ②Tie theme to B Story ③Seal with Moment of Clarity。
tags: [theme, b-story, structure, meaning]

id: p03
title: No Double Mumbo Jumbo — 一部电影只接受一种魔法
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "I propose that as an audience we can only buy one piece of magic per movie. You cannot have aliens land and then be bitten by vampires and become both alien and undead."
summary: |
  一部电影只能接受一种"超自然/科幻设定"。叠加大法(DMJ)会让观众认知超载,导致故事混乱。
  反面案例: Dreamcatcher 同时使用 ESP+外星人+细菌战+预知未来(Quadruple Mumbo Jumbo)。
  Spider-Man 3 使用了四种魔法(四个反派的起源各不相同)。
tags: [world-building, consistency, audience-cognition]

id: p04
title: Empowerment vs Comeuppance — 前提的能量方向
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch1 Ideas and Concepts
source_quote: "Bruce is more of an 'empowerment' tale, Evan more a 'comeuppance' tale. And apparently in and amongst all the summer movie traffic, fun is always more fun."
summary: |
  故事前提给观众的能量方向分两种:Empowerment(英雄获得力量,观众跟着爽)和
  Comeuppance(英雄被教训,观众跟着受罚)。前者票房通常优于后者。
  Bruce Almighty 是 Empowerment(拥有神力),Evan Almighty 是 Comeuppance(被迫造方舟)。
tags: [concept, audience-reception, pitch]

---

id: p05
title: One Idea Rule — 一部电影只有一个核心想法
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch1 Ideas and Concepts
source_quote: "A movie is about 'one idea.'... finding the 'one idea' makes your story spine stronger."
summary: |
  一部电影只能有一个核心主题/想法。找到这个"one idea"让故事脊柱更强壮。
  主角必须学习一个教训——选择一个——然后通过 Theme Stated 预先埋下,在结尾呼应。
  一旦找到主题,可以在主题框架内编织多条意义线索,但核心只有一个。
tags: [concept, focus, theme]

id: p06
title: Fun and Games = Your Pitch
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "Fun and Games = Your Pitch! The 'Fun and Games' section contains the 'promise of the premise' and that means it's also the poster of your movie."
summary: |
  Fun and Games 段落(第二幕开头)就是你的电影海报、你的 pitch、你的"前提的承诺"。
  如果你在寻找电影是什么,或情节的转折点在哪里,这个信息是关键。
  Minority Report 中 Tom Cruise 发现嫌疑人就是自己——这就是 logline。
tags: [structure, fun-and-games, pitch, concept]

id: p07
title: Shard of Glass — All Is Lost 时刻的性格碎片
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "That's the 'shard of glass,' that sharp-edged incident, bad behavior, tough truth or wrong done and absorbed that the hero swallowed a long time ago. Skin has grown up around its hard corners."
summary: |
  英雄在 All Is Lost 时刻被迫面对的、长期被忽视的丑陋真相或性格缺陷。
  这个"碎片"被皮肤覆盖,深埋在英雄体内,必须被拔出来审视才能完成转变。
  Alien: Sigourney 发现公司把他们当牺牲品; Notting Hill: Hugh Grant 发现自己才是势利鬼。
tags: [character, all-is-lost, transformation, flaw]

id: p08
title: All the Way Back — 把英雄推到最底层
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "If your story doesn't feel full enough, big enough, or quite on track, don't check Act Three — think about Act One."
summary: |
  如果第三幕不够有力,问题往往在第一幕——英雄没有被推到足够低的起点。
  开场和终场必须是"相反的快照",英雄的弧线越大越好。
  The Savages 中 Laura Linney 从专业迷茫+糟糕情妇+被兄弟控制 → 剧作上演+和解+获得自由。
tags: [structure, act-one, hero-arc, diagnosis]

id: p09
title: Primal Concept — 概念必须在"原始人"层面可理解
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch1 Ideas and Concepts
source_quote: "What travels best is: primal. We sorta had them there with the comedian description, everyone likes Funny People, don't they? But what got this person's attention... was the primal part: Adam might be dying."
summary: |
  故事概念必须在"穴居人"层面可理解:生存、性、饥饿、死亡、父亲、母亲、孩子。
  Meet the Parents 之所以有效,是因为"第一次见岳父母"是原始人就懂的情境。
  Jaws 有效是因为"别被吃掉"不需要上学就能理解。
tags: [concept, primal, marketing, universal]

id: p10
title: Irony as Hook — 反差才是卖点
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch1 Ideas and Concepts
source_quote: "What's 'ironic' about Erin Brockovich is not the plot... but the fact that the person doing the crusading is the very last person on Earth who would be called to this duty."
summary: |
  概念的"hook"不是情节本身,而是反差/irony。Erin Brockovich 的 irony 不是"揭露公司黑幕",
  而是"做这件事的人是地球上最不可能做这件事的人"。
  Irony 不仅吸引注意力,还暗示了英雄的转变和挑战的规模。
tags: [concept, irony, hook, logline]

id: p11
title: Title Must "Say What It Is" — 标题必须说清楚
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch3 Titles & Loglines
source_quote: "A good title must 'say what it is!' and yet give us a fresh, intriguing invitation to your party that gives us a hint of the type and tone of the festivities."
summary: |
  好标题必须"说它是什么",同时提供新鲜、引人入胜的邀请。
  反面案例: State of Play(看完电影也不知道标题指什么), Body of Lies(通用),
  For Love or Money(适用于任何电影的万能标题)。
  如果你不能命名它,标题不能直接导向故事,可能故事本身有问题。
tags: [title, concept, marketing, communication]

id: p12
title: Transform or Die — 开场与终场必须相反
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "All stories are about transformation... the opening image (the snapshot of the world BEFORE) of a movie script has to be the opposite of the final image (the snapshot of the world AFTER.)"
summary: |
  所有故事都关于转变。Opening Image 和 Final Image 必须是完全相反的"快照"。
  Act Two 是"The Transformation Machine"——英雄进去,但不会以原来的样子出来。
  如果变化不够大,说明故事没有真正发生。
tags: [structure, transformation, core-principle]

id: p13
title: Make the Bad Guy Badder — 反派越强英雄越大
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch2 Themes
source_quote: "'Make the bad guy badder' is surefire screenplay physics that guarantees your hero will be bigger, too."
summary: |
  "让反派更坏"是确定性的剧本物理法则——保证英雄也更大。
  Indiana Jones 1和3有纳粹(明确的恶),2和4的邪恶模糊,因此1和3更令人难忘。
  反派的质量直接决定了英雄的弧线规模和观众的情感投入。
tags: [villain, hero, conflict, stakes]

id: p14
title: Scene as Module of Drama — 每场景是迷你故事
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch7 Dialogue
source_quote: "I like to say that every scene is a mini-story — complete with 15 Beats that show change... the scene hero has to have a tangible goal."
summary: |
  每个场景都是一个"戏剧模块"——有自己的15个节拍和变化。
  场景英雄必须有具体目标;谁或什么在对抗英雄?找到动机就找到了白热化的冲突。
  如果角色在"谈论他们在做的事",需要紧急分诊。
tags: [scene-writing, dialogue, structure, micro]

id: p15
title: Bad Dialogue Test — 盖住名字测试
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch7 Dialogue
source_quote: "Try the Bad Dialogue Test: Cover up the names of characters speaking and see if you can tell who's talking by the way he or she talks."
summary: |
  盖住角色名字,看能否通过说话方式分辨谁在说话。
  如果不能,说明角色声音没有区分。十个人走进房间说"Hi!"应该有十种不同的方式。
  问题根源:角色是作者的"代言人"而非独立个体。
tags: [dialogue, character-voice, diagnostic]

id: p16
title: Catalyst ≠ Break into Two — 被动事件 vs 主动选择
type: principle
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "the Catalyst (something happening TO the hero on page 12) and the Act Break (something the hero actively DOES to take us into a new world) is crucial to see."
summary: |
  Catalyst(第12页发生的"对英雄发生的事")和 Break into Two(英雄"主动做的事"进入新世界)
  是两个不同的节拍,不能混淆。前者是被动接受,后者是主动选择。
  混淆两者会导致第一幕结构混乱——Setup 部分最常见的问题。
tags: [structure, act-one, catalyst, diagnosis]
