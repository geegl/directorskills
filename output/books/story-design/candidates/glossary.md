# glossary.md — 术语提取候选

---

id: g01
title: BS2 (Blake Snyder Beat Sheet)
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "The 15 beats of the BS2... These very same beats can be found in all successful stories."
summary: |
  15个故事节拍的序列模板。不是"三幕结构"的标签,而是精确到页数的可操作检查清单。
  从 Opening Image (p1) 到 Final Image (p110),每个节拍有明确的功能和页数位置。
tags: [structure, beat-sheet, core-term]

---

id: g02
title: Save the Cat (术语)
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "The 'Save the Cat!' beat... is that moment when we meet its hero and he does something 'nice' that makes us like him."
summary: |
  英雄出场时做的一件让观众喜欢他的事。变体:Kill the Cat(被同情),by proxy(通过他人推荐)。
  不一定非得是"救猫",可以是任何建立观众认同的小动作。
tags: [hero, identification, core-term]

id: g03
title: Fun and Games
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "The 'Fun and Games' section contains the 'promise of the premise' and that means it's also the poster of your movie."
summary: |
  第二幕开头的"前提的承诺"段落,对应电影海报和 pitch。
  不是"有趣的场景"的泛称,而是特定的结构位置——它是观众来看这部电影的原因。
  Minority Report: Tom Cruise 发现嫌疑人是自己。
tags: [structure, fun-and-games, core-term]

id: g04
title: B Story / Helper Story
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch2 Themes
source_quote: "The B Story is the 'helper story,' and it helps the heroes of the A tale transform."
summary: |
  帮助英雄理解主题的支线,通常是爱情线。
  不是"支线剧情"的同义词,而是主题讨论的载体。
  在 page 30 引入,在 Midpoint 和 Break into Three 与 A Story 交叉。
tags: [structure, b-story, theme, core-term]

id: g05
title: Midpoint
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "If you can crack the Midpoint, you've cracked the story... It's where there's either a 'false victory' or a 'false defeat.'"
summary: |
  故事的神经中枢,包含:false victory/defeat、stakes raised、time clocks、
  Bad Guys learn Good Guy's secret、boy-girl kiss (Sex at 60!)。
  不是"故事中点"的时间标签,而是多重功能叠加的关键节拍。
tags: [structure, midpoint, core-term]

---

id: g06
title: All Is Lost
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "The 'All Is Lost' moment on page 75 is becoming the most important part of the story... forced to face an ugly truth about himself."
summary: |
  英雄"比开场时更糟"的时刻,通常伴随"死亡气息"(whiff of death)。
  不只是"低谷",而是旧我必须死去的"茧期"入口。
  英雄被关进监狱、被驱逐、被解雇、被遗弃、或导师死亡。
tags: [structure, all-is-lost, core-term]

---

id: g07
title: Dark Night of the Soul
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "What follows immediately after is natural... the Dark Night of the Soul moment."
summary: |
  All Is Lost 之后英雄的困惑期。不是"悲伤",而是介于旧死与新生之间的"空窗"。
  蛹期——既不是毛虫也不是蝴蝶。必须跟在 All Is Lost 之后,不能在其前。
tags: [structure, dark-night, core-term]

---

id: g08
title: The Board
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "we put it up on the Board... and proceed to beat on it until we get the 40 scenes you need to start writing."
summary: |
  40张索引卡的场景规划系统,分4行(Act1/2a/2b/3)。
  不是"大纲",而是可视化的场景级故事拆解工具。
  "如果你能 pitch 全部40卡,你就准备好写了。"
tags: [structure, scene-planning, tool, core-term]

---

id: g09
title: 10 STC! Genres
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch4 Genres
source_quote: "most great stories fall into 10 story types — and each follows patterns that audiences love to see."
summary: |
  10种故事原始冲突类型。不是按题材分类(如"科幻""喜剧"),而是按故事的"原始冲突类型"分类。
  每种有固定的"必有元素"和"观众期待"。掌握类型就能预判观众想看什么。
tags: [genre, classification, core-term]

id: g10
title: Five-Step Finale
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "Think of every Finale in terms of 'storming the castle.' Step 1: plan. Step 2: execute. Step 3: trap. Step 4: dig deep down. Step 5: new plan wins."
summary: |
  Act Three 的5步"攻城"序列:计划→执行→落入陷阱→深挖→新计划获胜。
  不是"高潮"的泛称,而是有明确步骤的终局设计方法。
tags: [structure, finale, act-three, core-term]

id: g11
title: Shard of Glass
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch5 Heroes
source_quote: "That's the 'shard of glass,' that sharp-edged incident, bad behavior, tough truth or wrong done and absorbed that the hero swallowed a long time ago."
summary: |
  英雄在 All Is Lost 时刻必须面对的、长期被忽视的丑陋真相/性格缺陷。
  不是"弱点",而是被"皮肤覆盖"的深层旧伤——必须被拔出来审视。
tags: [character, transformation, core-term]

id: g12
title: Synthesis (正-反-合)
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "You may call it Act One – Act Two – Act Three. I call it Thesis – Anti-thesis – Synthesis."
summary: |
  Act Three = 旧我(Act1) + 所学(Act2) = 第三条路。
  不是"大团圆",而是主角整合新旧自我的结构性时刻。
  Rose Dawson, Dudley 做爱时放 Bolero。
tags: [structure, three-acts, philosophy, core-term]

id: g13
title: Empowerment vs Comeuppance
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch1 Ideas and Concepts
source_quote: "Bruce is more of an 'empowerment' tale, Evan more a 'comeuppance' tale."
summary: |
  前者是主角获得力量的故事(观众爽),后者是主角被教训的故事(观众闷)。
  不是"正面/负面结局",而是故事前提给观众的能量方向。
tags: [concept, audience-reception, core-term]

id: g14
title: Double Mumbo Jumbo (DMJ)
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "I propose that as an audience we can only buy one piece of magic per movie. I call it Double Mumbo Jumbo."
summary: |
  一部电影只能接受一种"魔法"设定。不是"逻辑不一致",而是观众认知负荷的极限。
  反面: Dreamcatcher (Quadruple Mumbo Jumbo), Spider-Man 3。
tags: [world-building, consistency, core-term]

id: g15
title: Catalyst
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "the Catalyst (something happening TO the hero on page 12) and the Act Break (something the hero actively DOES)"
summary: |
  第12页发生的"对英雄发生的事"——被动事件,打破英雄的生活平衡。
  和 Break into Two 不同:Catalis 是被动的, Break 是主动选择。

id: g16
title: Theme Stated
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch2 Themes
source_quote: "The Theme Stated moment of a movie is vital; it's what your movie is about."
summary: |
  主题被说出的时刻,通常在第5页左右。
  通常由英雄认为"没什么可告诉我的"的人说出来,在当时看似不重要。
  Titanic: "Make each day count"。Planes Trains: "Like your work. Love your wife."
tags: [theme, structure, core-term]

id: g17
title: Opening Image / Final Image
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "the opening image (the snapshot of the world BEFORE) of a movie script has to be the opposite of the final image (the snapshot of the world AFTER.)"
summary: |
  开场快照和终场快照——必须是完全相反的。
  Liar Liar: 开场 Jim Carrey 是骗子,终场他不是。Sleepless in Seattle: 开场埋葬亡妻,终场和 Meg Ryan 走开。
tags: [structure, transformation, core-term]

---

id: g18
title: Break into Two / Break into Three
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "the Break into Two (something the hero actively DOES to take us into a new world)"
summary: |
  Break into Two: 英雄主动选择进入新世界(Act 1→Act 2 的转折)。
  Break into Three: 英雄在 Dark Night of the Soul 后决定走"第三条路"。
  两者都是英雄的主动选择,不是对英雄发生的事。
tags: [structure, act-breaks, core-term]

title: Touchstone Scene

source_chapter: Ch2 Themes

id: g27
title: Stasis = Death
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "Mansion-less, friend-less, tired of the grind — if things don't change, Bruce Wayne will 'die.'"
summary: |
  英雄在开场时的状态如果不改变就会"死亡"——物理上或精神上。
  Dark Knight: Bruce Wayne 无 mansion、无朋友、疲惫——如果不改变就会"死"。
  这是 Catalyst 之前必须建立的"为什么英雄必须行动"的理由。

id: g28
title: Whiff of Death
type: glossary
source_chapter: Ch6 Structure
source_quote: "the All Is Lost 'whiff of death' (Downey actually dies and comes back in this particular page 75) close at hand."
summary: |
  All Is Lost 时刻伴随的"死亡气息"。可以是实际死亡、导师死亡、关系死亡、旧生活死亡。
  Ironman: Downey 实际上在第75页死去又复活。
  所有故事本质上都是"毛虫变蝴蝶",All Is Lost 是蛹期的入口。
tags: [structure, all-is-lost, death, core-term]
